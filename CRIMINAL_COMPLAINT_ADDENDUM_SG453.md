## IV. CRIMINAL VIOLATIONS

### A. Obstruction of Justice - s. 139(2) CCC

**Elements:**
1. Wilfully attempts to obstruct, pervert, or defeat the course of justice
2. In a judicial proceeding

**Evidence:**
1. **Judicial Proceeding:** Active Supreme Court of Canada filings exposing:
   - DEA Agent Glenn Dutton's kidnapping conspiracy
   - Attempted murder via fraudulent Nunavut transfer (Document #000573)
   - Judicial corruption by Ontario Superior Court judges
   - Evidence tampering and spoliation
   - Five Eyes cyber attack authorization

2. **Wilful Obstruction:** Account suspension prevented:
   - Public dissemination of evidence
   - Access to archived evidence documents
   - Continued documentation of conspiracy
   - Witness protection through public disclosure

3. **Timing:** Suspension occurred during critical Supreme Court filing period

**Penalty:** Up to 10 years imprisonment

**Case Law:**
- *R. v. Beaudry*, 2007 SCC 5 - Obstruction includes suppressing evidence
- *R. v. Beauchamp*, 2015 ONCA 260 - Destroying or concealing evidence constitutes obstruction

### B. Mischief to Data - s. 430(1.1) CCC

**Elements:**
1. Destroys or alters computer data
2. Renders computer data meaningless, useless, or ineffective
3. Obstructs, interrupts, or interferes with lawful use of computer data
4. Obstructs, interrupts, or interferes with any person in lawful use of computer data

**Evidence:**
1. **Unauthorized Account Suspension:** GitHub terminated access to repository containing legal evidence
2. **Data Rendered Useless:** Francesco cannot access, update, or share evidence
3. **Interference with Lawful Use:** Evidence documentation for court proceedings is legitimate legal purpose
4. **"Clone Browser" Creation:** Francesco reports "clone Chrome browser created for routing" - potential technical manipulation

**Penalty:** Up to 10 years imprisonment

**Case Law:**
- *R. v. Tse*, 2012 SCC 16 - Unauthorized alteration of computer systems
- *R. v. McIsaac*, 2019 ONCA 918 - Mischief to data includes restricting access

### C. Conspiracy - s. 465(1)(c) CCC

**Elements:**
1. Agreement between two or more persons
2. To commit an indictable offence
3. In furtherance of the agreement

**Evidence:**
1. **Multiple Actors:**
   - 7 GitHub officials reviewed account
   - Unknown complainants filed reports
   - Potential law enforcement coordination
   - Potential judicial official involvement

2. **Indictable Offence:** Obstruction of justice (s. 139(2))

3. **Furtherance:**
   - Coordinated review process (7 officials)
   - Suspension decision without evidence
   - Refusal to provide disclosure despite legal demands
   - Continued obstruction (67 days and counting)

**Penalty:** 
- Up to 14 years imprisonment for conspiracy to commit obstruction
- **Life imprisonment** if linked to attempted murder conspiracy (Document #000573 Nunavut transfer plot)

**Case Law:**
- *R. v. Venneri*, 2012 SCC 33 - Structured facilitation constitutes conspiracy
- *R. v. Root*, 2008 ONCA 869 - Conspiracy requires agreement and furtherance

### D. Public Mischief - s. 137 CCC

**Elements:**
1. Makes a false statement
2. With intent to mislead
3. Causes peace officer to enter on or continue investigation

**Evidence:**
1. **False Statement:** Complainants allegedly claimed Francesco "harassed" or used "offensive language"
2. **No Evidence:** GitHub cannot provide specific examples despite repeated demands
3. **Intent to Mislead:** Reports filed to suppress evidence, not address actual harassment
4. **Investigation:** GitHub conducted investigation and took enforcement action

**Penalty:** Up to 5 years imprisonment

**Case Law:**
- *R. v. Cuerrier*, [1998] 2 SCR 371 - Fabrication of allegations
- *R. v. McRae*, 2013 SCC 68 - False statement must be knowingly false

**Applies to:** GitHub officials (if knowing)

**Elements:**
1. Knows that person has been party to offence
2. Receives, comforts, or assists that person
3. For purpose of enabling that person to escape

**Evidence:**

2. **Assistance:** Account suspension prevents evidence dissemination
3. **Purpose:** Enable perpetrators (148 individuals) to avoid detection and prosecution

**Penalty:**

**Case Law:**
