Technical Analysis: Where is the Compromise?

Possible Interception Points

1. WiFi Router Level

Likelihood: HIGH
Compromised router firmware could intercept/modify all WiFi traffic before it reaches the ISP. This would explain why cellular data (bypassing the router) works normally.

2. PC/Operating System Level

Likelihood: MEDIUM

3. ISP Level

Likelihood: MEDIUM
ISP could be performing deep packet inspection and modifying specific traffic. However, this wouldn't explain why cellular data (which also goes through an ISP) works normally.

4. Man-in-the-Middle (Active Interception)

Likelihood: HIGH
Sophisticated MITM attack at router or ISP level, with SSL/TLS interception capabilities. This would require state-level resources and technical capability.
